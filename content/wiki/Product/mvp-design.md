# MVP Design

## Overview
A minimum viable product (MVP) is the smallest version of a product that can be released to learn whether a hypothesis about user value is true. It is not a half-finished product but a deliberately scoped experiment designed to generate the most learning with the least effort. MVP design is a core practice in lean startup methodology and product thinking.

## Core Concepts
- **Core Value Proposition**: The single benefit the MVP must deliver to be worth testing.
- **Learning Goal**: The specific assumption the MVP is built to validate or invalidate.
- **Build-Measure-Learn**: The loop of building a small thing, measuring results, and learning.
- **Concierge MVP**: Manually delivering the experience to test value before automating.
- **Definition of Done**: The criteria that signal the MVP has answered its question.

## How It Works
Designing an MVP starts by identifying the riskiest assumption in a product idea—the belief that, if wrong, invalidates the whole direction. The team defines what evidence would confirm or refute that assumption and designs the cheapest way to gather it. This might be a landing page, a clickable prototype, or a manual service rather than a full build. The MVP is released to real users, results are measured against the learning goal, and the team decides to persevere, pivot, or iterate. Each cycle narrows uncertainty and shapes the next experiment.

## Advantages
- Validates ideas before committing significant resources.
- Speeds up the feedback loop between idea and evidence.
- Forces clarity about what truly matters in the product.
- Reduces wasted effort on features users don't need.

## Limitations
- Easy to confuse "minimum" with "low quality," damaging trust.
- Learning from small samples can be noisy and misleading.
- Stakeholders may pressure teams to ship an MVP as a final product.
- Requires discipline to stop adding features and start measuring.

## Example
A team hypothesizes that students want AI-generated study summaries. Instead of building a full app, they email summaries manually to ten students and track whether they open them and ask for more. The concierge MVP reveals demand before a single line of production code is written.
